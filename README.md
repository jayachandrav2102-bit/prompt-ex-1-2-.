CHAPTER 1:

FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPTS OF GENERATIVE AI:

     *Generative Artificial Intelligence refers to AI systems 
that are capable of generating new data rather than merely analyzing 
existing data. Traditional AI systems focus on classification, prediction, 
and decision-making tasks. In contrast, Generative AI focuses on 
creativity and content generation.
     *Generative AI models learn from large datasets and 
identify patterns, structures, and relationships within the data. Once 
trained, these models can generate new content that closely resembles 
the training data.


 Key concepts of Generative AI include:
- Data distribution learning
- Probability-based generation
- Neural networks
- Deep learning techniques
 Generative AI is widely used in fields such as education, 
healthcare, entertainment, finance, research, and software development.

CHAPTER 2:

GENERATIVE MODELS:

         *A generative model is a machine learning model 
that understands how data is generated and can produce new samples. 
These models attempt to capture the probability distribution of the 
input data.
          *Generative models differ from discriminative 
models. While discriminative models classify or label data, generative 
models create new data instances.
Generative models are the backbone of Generative 
AI and are responsible for producing realistic outputs.


CHAPTER 3:

TYPES OF GENERATIVE MODELS:

1.Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)
     *GANs consist of two neural networks: a 
Generator and a Discriminator. The generator creates fake data, 
while the discriminator evaluates whether the data is real or 
fake. Through competition, both networks improve.

2. Variational Autoencoders (VAEs)
      *VAEs encode data into a latent space and decode it 
to reconstruct data. They introduce randomness, allowing the 
generation of new samples.

3. Autoregressive Models
      *These models generate outputs step by step by 
predicting the next element in a sequence.

4. Diffusion Models
      *Diffusion models add noise to data and then 
learn to remove it, generating high-quality outputs.
